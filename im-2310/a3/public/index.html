FIND THE FACE


HUGH F


In my design, I explore the idea of growing agency. Clicking one of the distinct ‘happy’ faces before the time bar grows to its deadline will let the user progress to the next level. Each level makes the amount of distinct faces decrease, giving the user a sense of growing agency as they accomplish the interaction levels. By the end, the user’s sense of agency has grown in proportion to the difficulty of the interaction.
I set out on my design journey ideating upon the previous experiments in Assignment 2 of my Interactive Media course at RMIT. Having grasped certain Javascript functions, such as CSS attribute retrieval and SetTimeout(), I was able to build code with varying aspects as follows:

Time Bar

The time bar grows upward as time runs out, forcing the user to undergo a growth in their personal sense of agency in order to further their "exploitation of the procedural and participatory properties" of the interaction. (Murray, 2012) I made the time bar red in order to foster a sense of urgency, whilst making the remaining theme a light blue to contrast the time bar.

Face (.png) Generation

Each "happy" face image indicates interactivity when the user's cursor hovers over it, as evidenced by the cursor changing. Upon clicking a ‘happy’ face, the next level begins. With a growing scarcity of ‘happy’ faces in each level, along with a shorter time to find the face, the probability of successfully completing the level becomes narrower. The user’s sense of agency increases upon their accomplishment. The locations of the faces on the page are designed to pop up randomly over a set amount of time. The randomness affords the ability to repeat the interaction at a consistent level of difficulty in order to keep the growing sense of agency constant.

Page Transitions

Each level begins with a prompt that is designed to discourage the user’s agency so as to force them to be more focused upon entry to the next level. In other words, their "capacity of exerting power" (Merriam-Webster 2023) needs to grow in order to complete the interaction. Every button has a color transition upon hover to indicate interactivity.

Javascript Optimisation

Initially I had 10 levels, all with their own HTML pages. The interaction was smoothly loading between pages. However, it was made apparent that the user could easily re-load the HTML page and replay any level at any time. In the interest of exploring the idea of growing agency it was crucial that I made the user’s agency solely dependent upon the designed interaction. In order to make this happen, I put all of the interaction’s HTML code onto one page. I then built a function that set a desired element’s display to be visible, while setting the display of other elements to ‘none’. Now I have better control over the user’s interaction. This approach in design does not afford cheating, and helps foster a sense of growing agency.
